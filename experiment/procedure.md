#### These procedure steps will be followed on the simulator

#### Specific Gravity of Cement :

1. When you open specific gravity of cement experiment, a window will open as shown.<br>
<img src="images/sp_cement_1.PNG"><br>

2. Move mouse pointer over the description to read and recall the basic terms. Now click on Le-Chatelier flask to place it in a weighing machine and then click on NEXT Button at the bottom right corner to proceed.<br>
<img src="images/sp_cement_2.png"><br>

3. Here the empty weight (W<sub>1</sub>) of the flask will be obtained, then select answer to the question displaying, then click NEXT Button. <br>
<img src="images/sp_cement_3.PNG"><br>

4. Now click on the stopper to open it and then click on hand with cement setup to add cement (almost 64g) into the flask.<br>
<img src="images/sp_cement_4.PNG"><br>

5. Click on the stopper again to place it in its position and then click on Le-Chatelier flask to place it on weighing machine, ie (W<sub>2</sub>), then click NEXT Button.<br>
<img src="images/sp_cement_5.PNG"><br>

6. Now open the stopper by clicking on it then click on beaker to pour kerosene into it (W<sub>3</sub>).<br>
<img src="images/sp_cement_6.PNG"><br>

7. Repeat the above steps and add only kerosene to the flask (W<sub>4</sub>), then Click NEXT Button. <br>
<img src="images/sp_cement_7.PNG"><br>

8. Same way add water to the flask and then click on it to place it in weighing machine (W<sub>5</sub>). <br>
<img src="images/sp_cement_8.PNG"><br>

9. Calculate the specific gravity value of cement from the given observation and for formula click on FORMULA, enter the value and then click on CHECK.<br>
<img src="images/sp_cement_9.PNG"><br>

10. Repeat the same steps in further trials to get the average specific gravity of cement, enter the value and click CHECK to verify the answer.<br>
<img src="images/sp_cement_10.PNG"><br>

#### Specific Gravity of Fine Aggregates :

1. When you open specific gravity of fine aggregate simulation, a window will open as shown. <br>
<img src="images/Sp_fine_ag_1.PNG"><br>

2. Move mouse pointer over the description to read and recall the basic terms. click on NEXT Button at the bottom right corner to proceed.<br>
<img src="images/Sp_fine_ag_2.PNG"><br>

3. Here select the type of the sand to test. Then click NEXT Button. <br>
<img src="images/Sp_fine_ag_3_.PNG"><br>

4. Click on pycnometer to place it on the weighing machine to get weight (W<sub>1</sub>). Then click on the bottom cap first and then click on top cap to open it.<br>
<img src="images/Sp_fine_ag_4.PNG"><br>

5. Now click on the hand to pour sand into the pycnometer. Then click on the caps again to place it in position.<br>
<img src="images/Sp_fine_ag_5.PNG"><br>

6. Click on the pycnometer to place it in weighing machine to get the weight W<sub>2</sub> and select answer to the question displaying. Then click NEXT button.<br>
<img src="images/Sp_fine_ag_6.PNG"><br>

7. Repeat the same steps to pour water into it and then weigh it by clicking on pycnometer to get weight W<sub>3</sub>.  <br>
<img src="images/Sp_fine_ag_7.PNG"><br>

8. Measure the weight of pycnometer with water W<sub>4</sub>. then Click NEXT Button. <br>
<img src="images/Sp_fine_ag_8.PNG"><br>

9. Calculate the specific gravity value of fine aggregate from the given observation and for formula click on FORMULA, enter the value and then click on CHECK.<br>
<img src="images/Sp_fine_ag_9.PNG"><br>

10. Repeat the same steps in further trials to get the average specific gravity of fine aggregate, enter the value and click CHECK to verify the answer.<br>
<img src="images/Sp_fine_ag_10.PNG"><br>
